How We Work Together

The right conversation
at the right moment.

Each engagement is designed to meet you where you are - from a first diagnostic conversation to ongoing capital architecture partnership. No generic ESG products. No pressure. Just the thinking that sits upstream of every allocation decision that matters.

01
Entry Point

Capital Clarity Session

A focused 90-minute working session to map your capital stack, run it through four transition themes, and identify where your money is - and isn't - doing what you want it to. You leave with specific observations and a clear picture of where deeper work would matter most.

Many people find the session itself changes how they think about their next conversations with their advisors.

  • 90-minute 1:1 working session
  • Capital stack mapping across four transition themes
  • Identification of 2–3 specific misalignments and opportunities
  • Short follow-up note with key observations and suggested next step
Schedule a Conversation For wealth holders and family offices at any stage of transition.
02
Deeper Work

Capital Design Intensive

For wealth holders ready to go beyond a single working session - and for families who want to build something together that will actually last. We go deeper: full capital stack review, values and legacy alignment, and a personalized capital design framework you can bring to your advisors and hand to the next generation. What emerges is a shared vision that is genuinely co-created, not inherited.

Choose your format:

Virtual — Five touchpoints over 30 days

  • Intake call: Orientation and preparation - so we arrive at Session 1 ready to go deep
  • Session 1: Transition readiness review - full capital stack review and assessment
  • Session 2: Individual values and money mindset work
  • Session 3: Collective vision session - weaving individual clarity into a shared legacy framework
  • Integration call: Reflections, next steps, and how to bring this into your advisor conversations
  • Personalized Capital Design Framework
  • Money Manifesto - a living clarity document you keep, build on, and share

In-Person — 1.5–2 days

An immersive arc designed for the depth this work deserves. Includes preparation calls before we arrive so that every hour together is spent on what matters most. Timing and structure are calibrated to your family - the format below is based on a small family and will be adjusted accordingly.

  • Pre-work: One or two preparation calls to orient each participant before we meet in person
  • Day 1: Transition readiness review, full capital stack work, and individual 1:1 sessions with each family member
  • Day 2 (half day to full day): Collective vision session - weaving individual clarity into a shared legacy framework, closing with your Money Manifesto and a prioritized path forward
  • Personalized Capital Design Framework
  • Money Manifesto - a living clarity document you keep, build on, and share

Next Generation Option (recommended)

Before the collective session, each family member - including teens and young adults - has their own private 1:1 working session. Not about money. About what they care about, what they want to build, and how they want to show up in the world right now.

In a moment when every generation is asking the same question - what do I do with all of this uncertainty? - young people often have more clarity than they're given credit for. The entry point is always their passion, their curiosity, their sense of agency. This working session helps them find and name it.

By the time the family comes together, everyone arrives with something real to contribute.

Why this work.
Why now.

The $84 trillion intergenerational wealth transfer is not a future event. It is happening in families, in conversations between parents and children, in portfolios that were built for one world and are now being asked to navigate another.

Most advisors are excellent at the technical work. What they're rarely equipped for is the upstream question: what is this capital actually for, and how do you design it to serve that purpose across generations?

That's the work I do. It draws on 25 years of capital markets experience, a decade of civic innovation practice, and a genuine belief that the people I work with are capable of making their capital a force for the world they want to leave behind.
